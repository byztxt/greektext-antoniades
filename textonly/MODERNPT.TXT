The following collation by Jussi Ala-Konni reflects the differences
between the the text of the electronic Antoniades 1904/1912 Patriarchal
Edition and the authorized 1904 text of the Ecumenical Patriarchate of
Constantinople, as posted at the internet site of the Greek Orthodox
Archdiocese of America in 2009-2010, from which site the following
description is quoted:

"The Greek New Testament displayed is the authorized 1904 text of the
Ecumenical Patriarchate of Constantinople. The Patriarchal text has
been made available courtesy of the Greek Bible society and was
digitized in XML in cooperation with the Greek Orthodox Archdiocese
Department of Internet Ministries, the Greek Bible Society and the
American Bible Society IT Department's OSIS project.

"The Open Scriptural Information Standard (OSIS) was developed by the
Bible Technologies Group in co-sponsorship with the American Bible
Society and the Society of Biblical Literature.

"The mission of the Bible Technologies Group is to maximize production,
distribution, access, use, impact, and preservation of the Bible and
related materials from all time periods."

The collation lists all differences, including typographic errors,
between the two texts as found in the comparison, except where
bracketed words appear in the electronic Antoniades 1904/1912 text.
These bracketed words, which correspond to small print in the original
1904/1912 Antoniades text, without exception appear as part of the
Patriarchal text but indistinct in type from the rest of the text.

The verse numbering corresponds to the electronic Antoniades 1904/1912
text.

Antoniades 1904/1912 text ] the Patriarchal text

An appended asterisk * indicates that the change from Antoniades
1904/1912 shows the Patriarchal text agreeing with the
Robinson-Pierpont Byzantine Textform 2005 edition.

In one case (Mr 7:16), the asterisk is surrounded by parentheses (*).
This indicates that the entire verse is lacking in the Antoniades
1904/1912 edition and though present in the Patriarchal edition, it
appears in brackets, unlike the Robinson-Pierpont Byzantine Textform
2005 edition, which has no brackets.
